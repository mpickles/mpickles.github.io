Digital Marketing Metrics and KPI Dashboard

Python, Jupyter Notebook, Power BI | Link to Project Github

ABOUT THIS PROJECT

In this project, I took marketing data from kaggle.com, cleaned it, and created a Power BI dashboard to explore and visualize insights into the data. The dataset contained marketing spending, revenue, profit, and metric data generated from different digital marking campaigns run by an Indian-based company. The dataset was put into a Jupyter Notebook where I used the pandas library to transform and clean the raw data. The data was then exported as .csv file and uploaded to Power BI, where I made the dashboard.

TLDR

  • The best campaigns are the youtube_blogger and facebook_retargeting campaigns
  • The campaigns that should be ended and their budget reallocated are:
    • facebook_lal
    • facebook_tier1
    • facebook_tier2
    • instagram_tier2
  • The campaigns that can be kept and improved upon are:
    • banner_partner
    • instagram_blogger
    • instagram_tier1

Totals Dashboard

Overall, the marketing campaigns where a success. The campaigns could be broken up into 4 different categories: Influencer, Media, Search, and Social. Influencer marketing campaigns were campaigns where the company paid influencer to promote products to their audience; Media refers to traditional advertising, in this case banner advertisements on websites; Search referred to promoting products on search engines, i.e. Google; Social referred to promoting advertisements on social media platforms such as Facebook and Instagram.

Return on Marketing Investment

The Return on Marketing Investment (ROMI) is a metric that measures measures the effectiveness of every dollar spent on marketing. The higher the ROMI, the more effective our marketing spending.

Category Average ROMI
All 40.76%
Influencer 170.00%
Media 24.23%
Search 19.14%
Social 7.64%


All marketing channels together had an average ROMI of 40.76%. This indicates that not every campaign was profitable. For every $1.00 spent on marketing, we profited only $0.4076, which is wasteful. Looking at average ROMI by the marketing categories, we see that:

  • Media, Search, and Social Marketing all had ROMIs of under 100%, meaning they were not very effective.
  • Influencer Marketing was very effective, generating an ROMI of 170%

Conclusions

From the data, we can conclude that the best marketing campaigns were youtube_blogger and facebook_retargeting. The campaigns were constantly leading all metrics. We shouldn't ignore the other campaigns. Its clear to see that a majority of the social media campaigns are ineffective and causing the company to lose money.

The youtube_blogger and facebook_retargeting should be kept and continued to have money allocated to them. The have good ROMIs and are very cost efficient, bring in a majority of the orders.

The google_hot campaign had the 3rd highest ROMI and Conversion Rate and 2nd highest CTR. It is in the middle of pack in terms of CAC, so its a campaign that should also be kept.

The instagram_tier1 campaign is the only other social campaign that should be kept. It has a higher ROMI of 64.6%, average CTR of 0.50 and conversion rate of 11%, and middle to higher CAC. What sets it apart from the instagram_tier2, facebook_tier1, and facebook_tier2 is that its ROMI is positive. The others have very similar CTR, CAC and conversion rates, but they have negative ROMI, thus should be ended.

The instagram_blogger and banner_partner campaigns are the last two campaigns that I believe should be kept. The have okay ROMIs of 33% and 24%, great conversion rates of 15%, and lower CAC of $52.76 and $40.06, respectively. instagram_blogger has a much higher CTR of 0.98% opposed to banner_partner's CTR of 0.13%, but the lower CAC and positive conversion rates and ROMI convince me that the campaigns should be kept, although they may need some work the better the ROMI.

The facebook_lal campaign should be ended. It has the worst ROMI and the highest CAC. While the conversion rates and CTR are good, the fact that it's ROMI is close to -90% and CAC is $105.78, the next highest CAC being facebook_tier2 at $83.31, it is a very cost inefficient that should be ended and its budget reallocated to other campaigns.

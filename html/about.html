MY ACADEMIC JOURNEY

In the summer of 2015, I was a bored 15-year-old, and thought it would be fun to learn how to code a 2D platformer game, igniting my interest in computer science. This passion continued through high school, where I found an interest in mathematics and was granted the opportunity to pursue independent engineering projects for my science classes, fostering my self-paced learning.

Upon entering UCSD in 2019, initially undeclared, I gravitated towards economics but soon realized I had a stronger fascination with the mathematical aspects. This led me to cahnge my major to Applied Mathematics, where I honed my skills in statistical learning, numerical optimization, and data science, shaping my academic journey around a thirst for knowledge and problem-solving.

Outside of school, I spent my free time immersed in coding. I delved into various computer science and machine learning concepts. I learned Python so I could understand and solve leetcode problems, as well as explore data and build machine learning models. I also spent time learning how to not only build websites but also focus on UX design for websites. I also explored topics like blockchains and quantum computing. I was a dedicated learner, often auditing courses on Coursera and diligently taking notes to expand my knowledge.
